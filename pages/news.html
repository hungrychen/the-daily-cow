Greyhound Bus Crashes on 101-N in South San Jose

By ------ and ------
January 19, 2016


BREAKING NEWS -- Bay area residents experienced a slow morning commute today when at 6:38 am, a Greyhound bus carrying twenty people flipped onto its side while moving from 101-N to the 85-N carpool ramp in south San Jose.

The bus had departed from Los Angeles at 11:30 pm on Monday night and was scheduled to reach Oakland at 8:10 am. According to drivers, the bus looked like it was making a safe and normal entrance to the 85 ramp when it hit impact barrels located at the divider between the exit ramp and freeway. It ended up landing on its side. No other vehicles were involved.

The tragic accident took the lives of two women and injured ten others, including the driver. It was reported that the two women were thrown through the front windows of the bus. Greyhound buses are equipped with seatbelts but passengers are not required to use them. The injured were transported to local hospitals and a few have been discharged.

Investigators are unsure of the reasons for the crash. Throughout the day, lanes surrounding the accident were closed while both CHP and the National Transportation Safety Board conducted investigations. An official from the District Attorney's office also talked with the driver, 58-year old Gary Bonslater. Bonslater said that he was tired but did not actually fall asleep. He will be asked to submit a blood sample for alcohol/drug testing. The bus was towed to a CHP yard at 5:00 pm, where investigations will continue.

Meanwhile, keep the passengers and their families in your prayers.
